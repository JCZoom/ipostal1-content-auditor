How does iPostal1 work?
  1. Choose a Location and Plan. Choose a physical address from 4,000+ digital mailbox locations in the U.S. and internationally. Then select the personal or business plan that is best for you, depending on how much mail you expect to receive.
  2. Checkout. At checkout, provide your contact information plus credit card or PayPal account. (PayPal is not available for mailbox locations in Canada.)
  3. Verification of Identity. After signing up for a location you will be assigned a personal mailbox number and receive instructions for verifying your identity to help prevent fraud:
    • For U.S. locations, the USPS requires the Form 1583 be completed, notarized, and filed with two forms of ID. Your mail center may allow you to sign in their presence for a small fee. To notarize your form without going to your mail center you must use our secure and convenient online notary provider for $25.
    • For locations outside the U.S., you will also need to complete a required identity verification process.
  4. Redirect Your Mail. To forward mail from U.S. addresses and receive mail at your new address, file a Temporary Change of Address form with the USPS and/or notify senders directly. To forward mail from addresses outside the U.S., check your local postal service procedures.

    Now you can:

  • View and Manage Your Mail from Anywhere 24/7. See your mail as soon as it is received. Use our free app or any computer to view an image of the outside of each mail item you receive. With a click or tap, tell your mail center what you want to do with each item: forward, scan, schedule a pickup, discard, or shred.
  • Receive Mail & Packages from Any Carrier. Because every iPostal digital mailbox address is a real street address, not a standard postal box, you can receive mail and packages not only from the USPS, but also from FedEx, UPS and other carriers.
  • Add Locations with Just a Click. It's fast and easy to add additional address locations for business or personal use. Manage mail for all locations from one login.

What is Form 1583? Why is it necessary?

The United States Postal Service (USPS) requires Form 1583 to verify the identity of the postal mailbox renter and to authorize the Commercial Mail Receiving Agency (CMRA) to receive mail on behalf of the postal mailbox renter.

A CMRA needs to have a properly signed and notarized Form 1583 and two forms of ID to give you access to or request tasks for your mail.

Can I file Form 1583 if I don't have a U.S. address?
Yes, you can and must file USPS form 1583, even if you don't have a U.S. address. You'll be required to notarize Form 1583 using our online notary service for $25.
Which ID's can I provide for Form 1583?

Two forms of identification are required, a Photo ID and a Proof of Address Document.

The Photo ID must have a clear photograph of the mail recipient.

Acceptable Photo IDs include:
  • Valid U.S. state driver's license
  • Valid U.S. state non-driver ID
  • U.S. passport
  • Foreign passport
  • Matricula Consular card (Mexico)
  • NEXUS card (Canada)
Acceptable Proof of Address Documents include:
  • Current lease
  • Mortgage or deed of trust
  • Voter or vehicle registration card
  • Home or vehicle insurance policy
UNACCEPTABLE IDs include:
  • Social Security cards
  • Credit cards
  • Birth certificates
Do I have to get the Form 1583 notarized?

Yes. The USPS requires that the Form 1583 be notarized to help prevent fraud, protecting both you and the mail center. Many mail centers allow you to sign the form in their presence, in which case notarization may not be required. To notarize your form without going to your mail center, you need to use our online notary service for $25. Our online notary is secure and reliable, ensuring that your Form 1583 can be verified. We offer substantial discounts for additional notarizations during the same session.

Why do I have to get Form 1583 notarized?
